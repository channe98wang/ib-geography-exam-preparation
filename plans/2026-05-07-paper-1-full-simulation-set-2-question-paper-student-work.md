Congo has one of the worst cases of malnutrition in the world, and
also has many instances of respiratory illnesses and vector borne diseases. In

the lack of access to the adequate nutrients needed for a healthy body. The
main reason for this is due to the food insecurity, that was a result

there are significant disparities between the capital city Kinshasa, and the
rest of the country, when it comes to infrastructure quality and food

for the treatment of these illnesses, and much of the healthcare profesionals are

these diseases.

In addition to sharing common factors, undernutrition is linked to infectious diseases
through the fact that those who do not get adequate nutrients are more

systems do not have the necessary nutrients needed to be healthy.

diseases.

In general, infectious diseases link to malnutrition due to the similar

14. a) traffic congestion is a major problem that increases the idling time of
cars and thus the amount of CO2 and NO2 being released in the air
and significantly decreases economic productivity. In large cities, hours upon
hours of time can be spent sitting in traffic, and these hours, which
could have gone to increasing economic growth in the area is wasted. New
York City suffers greatly from this issue, and it ranks at around the
top when it comes to being the city that loses the most amount
of GDP to traffic congestion. Thus, the city has implemented many
strategies to help reduce the number of cars on the road.

The main strategy was putting tolls on cars. Cars that would pass
major bridges and tunnels were charged, and recently as of January
2026, all vehicles that drive into manhatton from below 60th
Street are subjected to an increased toll. These tolls are incredibly
effective at decentivising the use of cars, which significantly reduced
the number of vehicles entering the toll zone, and thus, the travel
times. However, they create a financial burden for drivers, and
may divert the congestion to alternative routes, but these concerns
have yet to materialize in New York City.

Another strategy adopted by the new york government is better investment in
its public transportation. All of the revenue made off of its tolls is going to
the MTA, which is who's in charge of providing public transportation in NYC. these
funds will be used to upgrade the already expansive NYC transportation system,
which further incentivises the use of these services and thus reduces the amount of
cars on the road. The NYC subway system is already incredibly expansive and
has 4.6 million people using it for their day to day commute instead of
cars, However, the infrastructure is not even, and while areas like midtown manhattan
have a lot of station and lines, areas like Queens have less subway access, which
makes this a less attractive option for commuting.
