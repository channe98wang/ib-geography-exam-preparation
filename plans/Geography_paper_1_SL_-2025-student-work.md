12. a) Geographic factors play a large role in the diffusion of vector-
borne diseases. Whether it is geographic landforms, economic factors, or
climate, geographic factors are incredibly important, with geographic features
and climate being the most important, followed by economic factors.

Geographic features and climate are the most important factors when it
comes to the diffusion of vector borne disease. this is due to the fact
that the vectors carrying these diseases are often sensitive to specific
climates and environmental conditions. For example, mosquitos, which transmit
malaria, favor standing water and warm temperatures, which makes Malaria
the most prominent in tropical and subtropical areas. Malaria cannot be
found in extreme heat and it also cannot be found at extremely high
altitude. It is also transmitted by a mosquito that is not found in
specific parts of the world and favors tropical areas. This means that
If an infected person brought the malaria causing parasite to an area
that did not house that specific mosquito type due to the area's climate
and geographic features, it would be impossible for diffusion of the
disease to occur, as there is not vector that can carry the disease to another
person.

Economic factors also play an incredibly large role in the diffusion
of vector borne disease. Areas with higher incomes and better economics generally
have more developped infrastructure, such as health and sanitation infrastructure, that
makes it more difficult for the disease to diffuse. If an area has better health
infrastructure, the liklihood of vaccines and treatments being available are significantly
higher, which helps contain the parasite, thus decreasing the amount of humans being infected,
which decreases the amount of mosquitos getting the parasite and thus, the rate at which
the disease diffuses. Additionally, better sanitation infrastructure creates less favorable
environments for mosquitos, as they love the standing water associated with poor
sanitation infrastructure. This allows less mosquitos to be in the area, thus limiting
the rate of diffusion.

Thus, both geographic features & climate, as well as economic factors, have a
huge role in the diffusion of vector-borne diseases. The geographic factors, like
temperature, the existence of bodies of water, are more important, as they often
dictate whether or not the appropriate vector or disease can exist in a specific
area, whereas economic factors can allude to the development of important
infrastructure, which dictates how fast the disease diffuses based on the infrastructure's
capacity to contain the virus.

14. a) Air pollution is an incredibly serious issue, and is primary cause of
global warming and the degradation of environmental systems. Many cities
are working to reduce air pollution using different strategies, with
some strategies being more effecive than others.

Shanghai is dealing with air pollution in a two ways. The first is
through improving their public transport system, and second is through
increasing the green spaces in its urban area.

Shanghai has one of the biggest transportation networks in the world.
It features the world's longest metro system, and its public transportation
network handles over 10 million daily riders. This reliance on public
transportation reduces the number of cars on the road, and thus, decreases
the amount of greenhouse gas emissions from the cars on the road. The
transportation network can house a significant amount of people, and thus can
replace many individual cars. It also reduces traffic congestion, which
reduces the idling time in cars, and thus decreases the amount of
compounds getting released into the air. However, these services may also
rely on fossil fuels. For example Shanghai's metro system relies
on electricity that is generating coal, which means that a
significant amount of CO2 is still be released into the air, especially
due to how much the metro operates, and how much distance
it covers each day. Additionally, transitioning from a large reliance
on individual cars to public transportation services has a high
initial cost, and building them is also detrimental to the environment.

Another way in which Shanghai is reducing air pollution is through increasing
the amount of green space in the city. The city is transforming much of
the urban areas to open spaces with many trees. They have a goal of covering
50% of the city in green space. Green space is incredibly effective at trapping
particulate matter, like dust and smoke, on leaves and bark, and absorbing
CO2, NO2, and SO2 through photosynthesis. They also help cool areas through
evapotranspiration and shade. However, trees have a relatively weak impact
on the reduction of air pollution, especially when compared to the impact of
directly reducing anthropogenic emissions. Additionally, the green spaces' capacity to trap
pollutants is directly related to seasons, with significantly less pollutants
being able to be trapped during winter where trees lose some or all
of their leaves.

Therefore, reducing air pollution is a significant priority that requires many
solutions. Increasing public transportation reduces individual car
emissions but may be costly and still emit a significant amount of greenhouse
gases. Increasing green space is great as they act as natural filters
for the air by absorbing pollutants, but they are seasonally dependent
and have less significant effects.
