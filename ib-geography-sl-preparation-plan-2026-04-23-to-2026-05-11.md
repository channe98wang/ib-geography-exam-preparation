## Daily Study Format

### Weekdays

Use the same structure each weekday:

- `Audio 1`: `12 min`, about `2,160 words`
- `Audio 2`: `12 min`, about `2,160 words`
- `Read / practice`: `25 min`

Recommended use:

- `Audio 1`: one `Paper 1` theme focus
- `Audio 2`: one `Paper 2` core focus or exam-skill focus
- `25 min block`: closed-book recall, then one short timed response, then error log

Good audio structure:

- `2 min`: key terms
- `4 min`: process / pattern / causes
- `3 min`: named examples or case studies
- `3 min`: one command-term style paragraph such as `explain`, `analyse`, `examine`, `evaluate`, or `to what extent`

### Weekends

Use `1.5 hours` as:

- `30 min`: closed-book recall and diagrams
- `30 min`: timed question or mini-mock
- `20 min`: self-check and fix gaps
- `10 min`: case-study drilling

## What To Read Or Practise In The 25-Minute Block

Rotate these rather than rereading notes passively:

- `5 min`: closed-book brain dump
- `10 min`: one short response, one graph description, or one essay plan
- `10 min`: compare against notes and add mistakes to the error log

High-value practice types:

- define, describe, explain, analyse, examine, evaluate, to what extent
- map, graph, and infographic interpretation
- turning one named example into a full case-study paragraph
- linking one core unit to another in `Paper 2`

## Case-Study Rule

For each theme and each core unit, aim to know:

- `2 strong named places`
- `4-6 precise facts or statistics`
- `1 management or response angle`
- `1 evaluative limitation`

That is enough to be usable in timed answers. More than that is helpful only if recall stays fast.
